# Editorial / Magazine

## Identity

Editorial design draws from print magazine lineage — Vogue, The New Yorker, Wallpaper*, Harper's Bazaar, Kinfolk. Strong typographic hierarchy, generous margins, structured grids inherited from print, a reverence for the page as composed surface. Refined serifs carry voice; whitespace frames content; photography earns its place.

The core move: treat the screen as a page. Columns, margins, running heads, and optical alignment matter.

## Variants

- **Classic Editorial** — Tall columns, justified text, drop caps, pull quotes, running heads. The digital magazine faithful to print.
- **Modern Editorial** — Asymmetric layouts, oversized display serifs, mixed column widths, strategic whitespace. Think Wallpaper*, Dwell, Cereal.
- **Fashion Editorial** — Full-bleed imagery, sparse text, couture typography. Vogue.com, SSENSE, AnOther Magazine.
- **Literary Editorial** — Serif-dominant, long-form emphasis, pull quotes as composition anchors. The New Yorker, Aeon, Longform, The Paris Review.
- **Scholarly Editorial** — Footnote-aware, citation-as-style, modular grid. Cabinet Magazine, academic digital journals, The Point.

## Typefaces to Reach For

**Display (serif):** GT Sectra, Tiempos Headline, Canela, Saol Display, Financier Display, Ogg, Domaine Display, Caponi, Reckless, Migra.

**Body (serif):** Tiempos Text, Source Serif, Libre Caslon Text, EB Garamond, Crimson Pro, Freight Text, Adobe Caslon.

**Companion sans (captions, marginalia):** GT America, Söhne, Untitled Sans, Neue Haas Grotesk — used sparingly in small sizes.

**Italic cuts** of the display face for pull quotes and emphasis; condensed sans for running heads (GT Pressura, Marr Sans Condensed).

## Palette Moves

- **Base:** Warm off-white or ivory. `#F8F4EC`, `#FAF7F2`, `#F2EDE0`, `#FEFBF5`.
- **Body ink:** Deep warm near-black. `#1A1714`, `#232226`, `#0E0D0B`. Cold grey reads institutional; editorial favors warmth.
- **Accent:** One saturated editorial voice — aubergine (`#4A1F3A`), brick (`#9A3B2E`), navy (`#1A2F4A`), oxblood (`#5C1A1A`), ochre (`#C08E3A`).
- **Tinted surfaces:** Side panels, pull-quote backgrounds — a 5% tint of the accent, or parchment.
- **Dark mode:** Warm near-black (`#0E0C08`) with paper-tinted text (`#E8E2D6`), not cold grey. Accent holds.

## Composition Moves

- 12-column grid; 2–3 text columns on wide widths; single generous column on narrow.
- Drop caps on opening paragraphs via `::first-letter` or a sized span — serif cap 4–6 lines tall, tracked tight.
- Pull quotes set in the display face, italic, pulled to the margin, optically aligned.
- Asymmetric headers: display runs wide across the gutter, subhead tucks into a narrow column.
- Full-bleed imagery as chapter breaks; photos carry weight with minimal crop.
- Running heads or folio numbers in condensed sans, small, positioned with typographic care.
- Hanging punctuation and optical kerning (`text-rendering: optimizeLegibility`, `hanging-punctuation: first`).

## Motion Flavor

Quiet and considered. 200–300 ms crossfades. Subtle hover states — underline slides in, opacity shifts, nothing hops. Scroll reveals as slow fade-up (translate 12–16px, opacity 0 → 1, 400 ms ease-out). Text animations rare and deliberate — one hero reveal per screen at most. No scroll hijacking; reading patterns rule.

## Depth & Texture

Flat surfaces dominate. When depth appears, it's subtle — a 1px hairline rule, a small shadow on a card that sits on the page. Paper grain at 2–4% opacity on the base layer reads as tactile without demanding attention. Photography carries texture by default; restraint elsewhere.
