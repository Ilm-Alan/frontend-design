# Typography Pairings

A working catalog of display/body pairings that succeed across archetypes. Each pairing names: display face / body face / archetype fit / use case / craft notes.

When picking a pairing: choose by archetype first, then by mood, then by stack availability (some require a license; web-friendly free alternatives noted where useful).

---

## Editorial / Long-form

**1. Tiempos Headline / Tiempos Text**
- **Archetype:** Editorial, Literary
- **Use:** Magazine-style long reads, journalism, literary publications
- **Notes:** A single family across display and body — the most coherent editorial choice. Tiempos was designed for editorial contexts; the contrast between headline and text cuts is calibrated for publication.

**2. GT Sectra / Söhne**
- **Archetype:** Modern Editorial, Refined
- **Use:** Contemporary editorial sites with restraint — Wallpaper-adjacent
- **Notes:** Sectra's flared serifs at display sizes pair against Söhne's clean grotesk body — the contrast carries the editorial-modern tension.

**3. Canela / Inter**
- **Archetype:** Luxury Editorial, Fashion Editorial
- **Use:** Magazine sites, premium content, fashion brands
- **Notes:** Canela's high-contrast serifs need scale to sing — set display at 80–120px. Inter as body keeps the page readable; when budget allows, swap Inter for Untitled Sans for a touch more character.

**4. Domaine Display / Freight Text Pro**
- **Archetype:** Luxury Editorial, Refined
- **Use:** Fashion houses, perfumeries, hospitality brand sites
- **Notes:** Both faces from Klim Type Foundry — they share a design philosophy. Domaine in italic for pull quotes is signature.

**5. PP Editorial New / Söhne**
- **Archetype:** Modern Editorial, Maximalist Editorial
- **Use:** Creative studio portfolios, editorial-leaning marketing sites
- **Notes:** PP Editorial New's Italic cut is the move — use it large, set tight. Söhne grounds the composition.

**6. Reckless Neue / Fragment Mono**
- **Archetype:** Contemporary Literary, Editorial-Tech
- **Use:** Long-form essays, design publications, literary tech blogs
- **Notes:** Reckless brings character without ornament; Fragment Mono for code/captions adds technical precision.

**7. Bodoni Moda / Freight Text**
- **Archetype:** Fashion Editorial, Classical
- **Use:** Vogue-lineage, fashion houses, period-appropriate
- **Notes:** Bodoni demands scale — use no smaller than 48px display. Hairlines need pixel snapping at smaller sizes.

## Swiss / International / Modern Sans

**8. Akzidenz-Grotesk / Akzidenz-Grotesk**
- **Archetype:** Classical Swiss, International Style
- **Use:** Pure Swiss design exercises, Müller-Brockmann homages
- **Notes:** A single family across all weights. Akzidenz Bold Extended for posters; Regular for body. Anything else dilutes the lineage.

**9. Helvetica Neue / Helvetica Neue**
- **Archetype:** Pure International, Classic Swiss
- **Use:** Faithful Swiss execution, brand-agnostic clarity
- **Notes:** Use only the original Linotype Helvetica Neue — Arial substitution kills the lineage. Weights 35 Thin through 95 Black.

**10. Söhne Breit / Söhne**
- **Archetype:** Contemporary Swiss, Tech-Modern
- **Use:** Modern brand sites, tech products, anywhere Linear/Vercel-adjacent
- **Notes:** Söhne Breit (extended) for display, regular Söhne for body. The most-imitated pairing in tech right now.

**11. Druk / GT America**
- **Archetype:** Editorial Swiss, Bold Modern
- **Use:** Editorial covers, hero sections that need to shout
- **Notes:** Druk Wide Super at 200pt is unmatched for impact. GT America rounds out the workhorse needs.

**12. GT Alpina / GT America Mono**
- **Archetype:** Premium Tech, Modern Editorial-Tech
- **Use:** Premium SaaS, design-conscious tech brands
- **Notes:** Alpina's italic cuts are exceptional — use them in subheads or callouts.

**13. Suisse Int'l / Suisse Works**
- **Archetype:** Swiss with Editorial Bridge
- **Use:** Brands wanting Swiss precision plus editorial warmth
- **Notes:** Suisse Works (the serif from the same family) for body adds reading warmth.

**14. Neue Haas Unica / Neue Haas Grotesk**
- **Archetype:** Contemporary Swiss with Warmth
- **Use:** Modern brand identity work, considered editorial-tech
- **Notes:** Both descend from the Helvetica lineage but with humanist touches that read warmer.

## Tech / Minimalist

**15. Söhne / Söhne Mono**
- **Archetype:** Tech Minimalist, Modern Swiss
- **Use:** Most contemporary tech products and SaaS
- **Notes:** The default pairing for "modern tech" in 2024–25. Söhne Mono for code, kbd elements, technical labels.

**16. Inter / JetBrains Mono**
- **Archetype:** Open-source Tech, Free Alternative
- **Use:** Developer tools, open-source projects, budget-conscious
- **Notes:** Both free. Inter has near-Söhne quality for free; JetBrains Mono is the dev-favorite mono.

**17. SF Pro Display / SF Pro Text**
- **Archetype:** Apple-Native, iOS/macOS
- **Use:** Apple ecosystem apps, web sites targeting Apple aesthetic
- **Notes:** Native on Apple platforms, free for those contexts. The optical sizes (Display vs Text) matter — use them correctly.

**18. Aeonik / Aeonik Mono**
- **Archetype:** Geometric Modern, Tech-Editorial
- **Use:** Tech brands wanting more geometry than Söhne
- **Notes:** More geometric than Söhne, less ornamental than Inter — sits between.

## Maximalist / Expressive

**19. Migra / Söhne Breit**
- **Archetype:** Maximalist Editorial, Contemporary Bold
- **Use:** Creative portfolios, brand sites with personality
- **Notes:** Migra's display cuts have wild character — the italic is unforgettable. Söhne Breit grounds it without dampening.

**20. PP Neue Machina / IBM Plex Mono**
- **Archetype:** Tech-Futurist, Maximalist Tech
- **Use:** Web3, crypto, modern tech with bold personality
- **Notes:** Machina is angular, mechanical, distinctive. Plex Mono provides technical body counterpoint.

**21. Eiko / GT America**
- **Archetype:** Expressive Branding, Maximalist
- **Use:** Brand identity work, creative agencies, expressive sites
- **Notes:** Eiko's contrast is dramatic — used in display only. GT America body keeps content readable.

**22. F37 Ginger / Söhne**
- **Archetype:** Charming Modern, Maximalist-Refined
- **Use:** Lifestyle brands wanting personality without zaniness
- **Notes:** Ginger's character details make brand identity feel hand-considered.

## Retro-Futuristic

**23. VT323 / JetBrains Mono**
- **Archetype:** CRT, Terminal Brutalist
- **Use:** Retro terminal aesthetics, hacker brands, throwback projects
- **Notes:** VT323 for hero/display, JetBrains Mono for readable code blocks. Free.

**24. Orbitron / Space Mono**
- **Archetype:** Synthwave, Sci-Fi
- **Use:** Music projects, gaming sites, retro-futurist brands
- **Notes:** Use Orbitron earnestly — its sci-fi reputation is *the* point in this archetype.

**25. PP Neue Machina / Space Mono**
- **Archetype:** Cyberpunk, Modern Retro-Futurist
- **Use:** Tech brands with cyberpunk inflection, gaming-adjacent
- **Notes:** Less period-locked than Orbitron; reads more "near future" than "1985."

---

## Art Deco / Period

**26. Poiret One / Bodoni Moda**
- **Archetype:** Art Deco Revival, Neo-Deco
- **Use:** Hotel/hospitality, perfumeries, theater branding
- **Notes:** Poiret's geometry says Deco; Bodoni body keeps it elegant. Letter-space Poiret display caps tightly.

**27. Bodoni 72 / Garamond Premier Pro**
- **Archetype:** Classical Editorial-Deco
- **Use:** Period publications, classical music, opera
- **Notes:** Both period-appropriate, both demanding of careful spacing and high-quality rendering.

---

## Organic / Natural

**28. Recoleta / Söhne Breit**
- **Archetype:** Organic, Warm Editorial
- **Use:** Wellness brands, food, lifestyle
- **Notes:** Recoleta's rounded serif counters cold geometry. Söhne Breit body adds workhorse reliability.

**29. Fraunces / Greycliff CF**
- **Archetype:** Contemporary Organic, Wellness
- **Use:** Wellness, lifestyle, modern craft
- **Notes:** Fraunces is variable — its softness or rigor adjusts via axis. Greycliff humanist sans pairs naturally.

**30. Caslon / Caslon**
- **Archetype:** Literary Classical, Traditional Editorial
- **Use:** Books, classical publications, period-appropriate sites
- **Notes:** A single family across the page. Adobe Caslon Pro is the standard.

---

## Lo-Fi / Mixed

**31. Mixed system stack (Helvetica + Times + Courier)**
- **Archetype:** Lo-Fi, Brutalist
- **Use:** Zine aesthetic, riot-grrrl-inflected projects, deliberate clash
- **Notes:** No license needed. Mix on the same page for maximum zine energy.

**32. Silkscreen / Courier Prime**
- **Archetype:** Lo-Fi Pixel, Vintage Computer
- **Use:** Pixel art zines, bandcamp small-label sites, retro projects
- **Notes:** Both free. Silkscreen for hero; Courier Prime for body keeps it readable.

## Notes on font sourcing

Premium foundries (Klim, Pangram Pangram, Commercial Type, Schick Toikka) license per-project. For client work, factor licensing into the budget. For personal/learning projects, the free alternatives below cover most archetypes:

- **Free editorial serif:** EB Garamond, Cormorant, Libre Caslon Text, Crimson Pro
- **Free Swiss sans:** Inter, Work Sans, IBM Plex Sans
- **Free monospace:** JetBrains Mono, IBM Plex Mono, Fira Code, Space Mono
- **Free expressive display:** Bebas Neue, Big Shoulders Display, Unica One

Subset fonts (only the weights used) and self-host where possible — Google Fonts works but Klim/Pangram self-hosting offers better control.
