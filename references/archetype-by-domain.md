# Archetype by Domain

When the brief specifies a domain or product type, anchor archetype selection here before settling on a choice. Editorial is the default-attractor for "design beautifully" across briefs — most domains below name archetypes whose grammar fits the surface better.

Each entry lists:
- **Reach for** — archetypes whose grammar most directly serves this kind of surface
- **Also consider** — archetypes that fit specific subgenres or yield in particular hands
- **Pulls against the brief** — archetypes whose grammar typically fights the surface's purpose

When the entry has no obvious match, infer from the brand's emotional register (use `palettes/by-mood.md` as a parallel guide).

---

## SaaS Analytics Dashboard / Observability

**Reach for:** Industrial (data-first, monochrome, signal color, instrument-panel grammar), Swiss (grid discipline, numerical composition as design)
**Also consider:** Editorial — only when data is genuinely framed as journalism (annotated charts, sidebar commentary, KPIs as headlines, narrative captions)
**Pulls against the brief:** Organic, Maximalist, Lo-Fi, Art Deco — warmth, spectacle, imperfection, or ornament work against scannability and trust

## Financial Dashboard / Trading Terminal

**Reach for:** Industrial (Bloomberg lineage, dark base, semantic green/red, dense tables, real-time numerics)
**Also consider:** Swiss (when the audience is institutional and clarity > density)
**Pulls against the brief:** Editorial, Organic, Maximalist, Art Deco — anything decorative or text-led reads as wasted screen real estate

## Developer Tools / Technical SaaS

**Reach for:** Industrial (terminal/CLI grammar, monospace, dark mode default), Swiss (Linear/Vercel-lineage modern tech)
**Also consider:** Brutalist (raw HTML aesthetic for indie tools, anti-design positioning), Minimalist (Apple-lineage premium tech)
**Pulls against the brief:** Organic, Art Deco, Retro-Futuristic — warmth, ornament, or period-locked grammar reads as decoration on a tool

## SaaS (General / Horizontal)

**Reach for:** Swiss (clarity, grid, modern tech), Minimalist (restraint, premium feel)
**Also consider:** Industrial (if data-centric), Maximalist (if differentiation through expressive hero is the strategic move)
**Pulls against the brief:** Lo-Fi, Brutalist (anti-design positions a B2B SaaS as untrustworthy)

## Documentation / Knowledge Base

**Reach for:** Swiss (rigorous typography, grid, clarity), Editorial (long-form reading, considered hierarchy)
**Also consider:** Industrial (technical documentation with code-heavy emphasis)
**Pulls against the brief:** Maximalist, Retro-Futuristic, Art Deco — visual ambition fights reading

## Forms / Single-Purpose Surfaces (Contact, Signup, Auth)

A form takes its archetype from the brand it serves — look up the brand's stated business type in this file first, then route through that domain's archetype. A "design studio contact form" routes through *Creative Agency / Design Studio Portfolio* (above), not through "form" as its own category. A "fintech signup" routes through *Fintech / Banking*. A "wellness app onboarding" routes through *Meditation / Wellness*.

When the brand or business type is genuinely unstated: pick the archetype that serves the form's *function* — Swiss for clarity, Minimalist for considered restraint. Editorial fits when the form is itself an editorial gesture (the "form-as-letter" move from a craft brand).

## When the brief is ambiguous

If no domain anchors the choice, ask: what's the surface's *primary act*?

- **Reading** → Editorial / Lo-Fi
- **Scanning** → Swiss / Minimalist / Industrial
- **Deciding / converting** → Maximalist / Swiss
- **Calming / focusing** → Organic / Minimalist
- **Discovering / browsing** → Editorial / Maximalist
- **Operating / controlling** → Industrial / Swiss
- **Celebrating / experiencing** → Maximalist / Retro-Futuristic / Art Deco
- **Trusting / signing** → Swiss / Minimalist / Editorial

Match the archetype to the act, not to the abstract "design beautifully."
