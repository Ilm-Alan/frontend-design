# Routing — domain to anchor

When the brief names a domain or product type, anchor selection routes here before deliberation. Each entry lists the anchors whose grammar most directly serves the surface, and the anchors whose grammar actively fights it.

The eight anchors: **Swiss, Industrial, Brutalist, Aurora Maximalism, Chaotic Maximalism, Retro-Futuristic, Organic, Lo-Fi**. There is no Editorial anchor. If a brief seems to want Editorial — magazine, book, literary journal — the closest anchors are Organic (warm reading register) or Lo-Fi (handmade zine register), or the brief may warrant producing default-register output without an anchor.

---

## SaaS Analytics Dashboard / Observability

**Reach for:** Industrial (instrument-panel grammar, semantic signal colors, monospace), Swiss (grid discipline, numerical composition)
**Fights the brief:** Organic, Aurora, Chaotic, Lo-Fi, Retro-Futuristic — warmth, spectacle, imperfection, or ornament work against scannability

## Financial Dashboard / Trading Terminal

**Reach for:** Industrial (Bloomberg lineage — black base, semantic green/red, tabular numerics)
**Fights the brief:** every other anchor — decorative or warm treatment reads as wasted screen real estate

## Developer Tools / Technical SaaS

**Reach for:** Industrial (terminal grammar, dark mode, monospace), Swiss (Linear / Vercel lineage, modern sans), Brutalist (indie-tool anti-design)
**Fights the brief:** Organic, Retro-Futuristic, Aurora — warmth, period-lock, or spectacle read as decoration on a tool

## SaaS (General / Horizontal)

**Reach for:** Swiss (clarity), Aurora Maximalism (if expressive hero is strategic)
**Fights the brief:** Brutalist, Lo-Fi, Chaotic (anti-design positions a B2B SaaS as untrustworthy)

## Documentation / Knowledge Base

**Reach for:** Swiss (rigorous typography, grid, clarity)
**Also viable:** Industrial (code-heavy technical docs)
**Fights the brief:** Aurora, Chaotic, Retro-Futuristic — visual ambition fights reading

## Forms / Single-Purpose Surfaces (Contact, Signup, Auth)

A form takes its anchor from the brand it serves. Look up the brand's stated business type here first, then route through that domain's anchor. A "design studio contact form" routes through *Creative Agency / Design Studio Portfolio*. A "wellness app onboarding" routes through *Meditation / Wellness*. A "punk label contact form" routes through *Punk / Hardcore Record Label*.

When the brand is genuinely unstated: pick the anchor that serves the form's *function* — Swiss for clarity, Organic for warmth.

---

## When the brief is ambiguous

If no domain anchors the choice, ask: what is the surface's *primary act*?

- **Reading** → Organic, Lo-Fi
- **Scanning** → Swiss, Industrial
- **Converting** → Chaotic, Aurora
- **Operating / controlling** → Industrial, Swiss
- **Calming / focusing** → Organic
- **Discovering / immersing** → Aurora, Retro-Futuristic
- **Celebrating** → Aurora, Chaotic, Retro-Futuristic
- **Trusting / signing** → Swiss
- **Refusing / positioning against the mainstream** → Brutalist, Lo-Fi

Match the anchor to the primary act, not to the abstract goal of "design beautifully."
